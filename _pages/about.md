I am an Associate Professor and Ph.D. Supervisor at the Intelligent Transportation Systems Research Center, Wuhan University of Technology. My research lies at the intersection of **Brain-inspired Vision, Computer Vision, Driving Behavior, Traffic Safety, and Intelligent Transportation Systems**. I am particularly interested in understanding how **visual perception** shapes driving decisions and how human cognitive mechanisms can inspire **safer autonomous driving systems**.

My research addresses a fundamental challenge in road transportation: achieving highly reliable, low-risk, and adaptive mobility by uncovering the **Perception–Decision–Behavior** chain. Inspired by advances in Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) and computational neuroscience, I investigate how visual information is transformed into risk perception, driving behavior, and proactive safety control.

My recent work has focused on three complementary directions. First, I study the neural and cognitive mechanisms underlying **drivers' visual perception**, revealing how optical flow cues and visual primitives contribute to distance perception, speed perception, and risk cognition. Second, I develop computational models to characterize the evolution of **driving risk** across multiple spatial and temporal scales, ranging from individual vehicle collision risk to traffic-flow-level risk propagation under uncertainty. Third, I design **vision-driven adaptive safety interventions**, including human-centered road visual guidance and proactive risk prevention strategies, to improve driving safety in complex traffic environments.

Over the past five years, I have led or contributed to multiple national research projects, including one sub-project of the National Key R&D Program of China and two projects funded by the National Natural Science Foundation of China. As the first or corresponding author, I have published over 70 papers, including two papers published on **Green Energy and Intelligent Transportation (GEITS, IF21.5)** and one on **eTransportation (IF18.7)**.



Research Vision
======
My long-term research vision is to develop **brain-inspired embodied intelligence** for safe and trustworthy autonomous driving. As intelligent vehicles evolve from isolated smart devices into embodied agents within human-centered transportation systems, a fundamental challenge is enabling them to **perceive, reason, and act** with human-like reliability in complex and safety-critical environments.

Inspired by human visual cognition and advances in artificial general intelligence (AGI), my research seeks to bridge neuroscience, computer vision, robotics, and transportation engineering. I aim to establish an end-to-end framework that integrates brain-inspired visual perception, risk-aware cognitive reasoning, and adaptive decision-making, allowing autonomous vehicles to understand dynamic traffic environments, anticipate evolving risks, and generate robust driving behaviors under uncertainty.

My work particularly focuses on challenging scenarios such as sharp curves, work zones, and low-visibility conditions, where current autonomous driving systems remain vulnerable. By combining human cognitive mechanisms with multimodal perception and cooperative intelligence, I seek to develop autonomous vehicles with enhanced embodied cognition and human-like driving capabilities. Ultimately, my goal is to build trustworthy intelligent transportation systems in which vehicles continuously learn, collaborate, and make safe decisions, advancing the next generation of embodied AI for mobility.
