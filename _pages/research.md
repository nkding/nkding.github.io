My research focuses on **human-centered artificial intelligence for intelligent transportation systems**, with an emphasis on understanding human perception, cognition, and decision-making in complex traffic environments. The long-term goal is to develop safer, more adaptive, and more human-like autonomous driving systems through brain-inspired intelligence and traffic safety research.

---

## Brain-inspired Vision and Visual Cognition

### Overview

This research investigates the visual perception and cognitive mechanisms underlying human driving. By studying optical flow, texture gradients, visual attention, motion perception, and visual cognition, we develop brain-inspired visual models for intelligent vehicles operating in complex traffic environments.

### Overview

This research explores embodied intelligence for autonomous driving by integrating brain-inspired perception, human-like decision-making, adaptive vehicle control, and vehicle-road-cloud collaboration to improve the intelligence and robustness of autonomous driving systems.
